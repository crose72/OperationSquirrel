About Operation Squirrel

Open-source robotics built for schools, students, and aspiring engineers.

The Founders

The Operation Squirrel development kit was created by Shaun Bowman and Cameron Rose, two software engineers who met while working together on General Motors’ Super Cruise (Level 2 autonomous) driving program. The drone autonomy stack that evolved into Operation Squirrel began as a side project—an effort to build an advanced, high-performance autonomous drone using affordable, off-the-shelf components without compromising engineering quality.

Shaun — Senior AI/ML Software Engineer (Torc Robotics)

Shaun is currently a Senior AI/ML Software Engineer at Torc Robotics, where he develops core communication and software infrastructure for commercial self-driving semi-trucks. His work supports real-time autonomy running across clusters of Jetson AGX Orin embedded computers.

Before joining Torc, Shaun worked as a Senior AI Engineer at General Motors, contributing to several core components of the Super Cruise Level 2 autonomous driving system. His work included developing mapping systems and lateral control planning (such as automatic lane changes), with software deployed across hundreds of thousands of production vehicles.

Earlier in his career, Shaun supported hybrid vehicle calibration and testing within GM’s advanced propulsion programs. These roles gave him extensive experience building real-time autonomous systems that meet rigorous automotive safety and reliability standards.

He is also an inventor on multiple patents related to autonomous vehicle algorithms and system design. Outside of industry projects, Shaun has built real autonomous platforms—including a Jetson-powered self-driving RC car capable of operating autonomously at 60 mph—demonstrating his practical experience in high-performance embedded autonomy.

Cameron — Embedded Systems & Vehicle Software Engineer

Cameron is an Embedded Software Engineer developing propulsion control and hybrid mode-selection algorithms for modern automotive platforms. His work centers on predictive control—software that anticipates future vehicle states and selects optimal operating conditions in real time—while meeting the strict reliability and deterministic timing requirements of safety-critical automotive systems.

Prior to his hybrid vehicle propulsion work, Cameron developed charge-time estimation algorithms for EVs including the GMC Hummer EV, Cadillac Lyriq, Escalade IQ, and Celestiq, and is an inventor on several related patents. His software is deployed across hundreds of thousands of production vehicles in daily use, where accuracy and dependability are essential.

He holds a degree in Mechanical Engineering and is currently pursuing a Master’s in Computer Science (Machine Learning) to deepen his background in autonomy, perception, and GPU-accelerated robotics. Cameron’s engineering experience spans firmware development, control algorithms, system integration, and hardware–software interfacing across complex vehicle architectures.

Outside of industry, Cameron co-developed the Operation Squirrel autonomy stack, integrating real-time perception, control, and embedded systems into a fully functioning autonomous drone platform. His work bridges low-level engineering with applied machine learning and robotics.

Why We Built This

We built Operation Squirrel as a learning tool for ourselves. It worked—so well that it led to high-value engineering job opportunities for us. We're confident it can do the same for you.

Operation Squirrel began as an attempt to build a drone capable of dynamic payload delivery using affordable hardware. As we worked, we kept noticing a bigger issue: students rarely get access to real engineering systems. The critical pieces—control loops, perception, timing, and real-world failure modes—are usually hidden behind simplified kits.

So we built a platform to change that—open, practical, and built on the same tools and constraints used in professional robotics. Dynamic payload delivery is only the beginning. We get you to the fun part instantly, while giving you fully open, adaptable software for whatever you want to build next.
